Every foot of fire sprinkler pipe must be properly supported to prevent sagging, movement, and failure. Hangers support the weight of the pipe and water. Bracing resists seismic and lateral forces. Both must be correctly designed, selected, and installed per NFPA 13.

---

## Hanger Types

### Clevis Hangers

The most common hanger for horizontal fire sprinkler pipe. A U-shaped metal strap wraps around the pipe, connected to a threaded rod that hangs from the building structure.

**Applications:** General-purpose hanging of horizontal mains and branch lines
**Sizes:** Available for all pipe sizes used in fire protection

### Ring Hangers

A metal ring that encircles the pipe, attached to a rod or strap:
- Allows more pipe movement than clevis hangers
- Sometimes used where thermal expansion must be accommodated
- Less restraint than clevis hangers (pipe can slide within the ring)

### Adjustable Band Hangers

Sheet metal bands that wrap around the pipe with an adjustment mechanism:
- Used for smaller pipe sizes (1" to 2")
- Lower cost than clevis hangers
- Less load capacity
- Not acceptable for mains in most applications

### Trapeze Hangers

A horizontal channel (strut/Unistrut) supported by two rods, carrying multiple pipes:
- Used where several pipes run parallel (common above corridors)
- Supports multiple pipe sizes on a single horizontal member
- Must be designed for the combined weight of all pipes
- Requires engineering for the channel size and rod diameter

### U-Bolt Hangers

A U-shaped bolt that wraps around the pipe and fastens through a structural member or channel:
- Simple and economical
- Used for smaller pipe sizes
- Limited to lighter loads

### C-Clamps

Clamp onto structural steel flanges to provide a hanging point:
- Used where drilling or welding to structure is not permitted or practical
- Must be listed for fire protection use
- Set screws must be tightened and often tack-welded to prevent loosening
- Retaining strap required to prevent the clamp from being knocked off

## Hanger Spacing

NFPA 13 specifies maximum distances between hangers based on pipe size:

| Pipe Size | Maximum Spacing |
|-----------|----------------|
| 1" | 12 feet |
| 1-1/4" | 12 feet |
| 1-1/2" | 15 feet |
| 2" and larger (steel) | 15 feet |
| CPVC (all sizes) | Per manufacturer's listing |

### Additional Hanger Locations

Hangers are required at specific points regardless of spacing:

- **Within 12 inches** of each change of direction (elbow, tee)
- **On each side** of a flexible coupling or expansion joint
- **Within 6 feet** of the last sprinkler head on a branch line (to prevent the pipe from flexing when a head is struck)
- **At the end of every arm-over** (drop or sprig connection to a sprinkler head over 12 inches long for steel, or per listing for CPVC)
- Wherever the pipe would be overstressed without support

### CPVC Hanger Spacing

CPVC systems have specific hanger requirements per the manufacturer's listing:

| Pipe Size | Typical Maximum Spacing |
|-----------|------------------------|
| 3/4" | 5.5 feet |
| 1" | 6 feet |
| 1-1/4" | 6.5 feet |
| 1-1/2" | 7 feet |
| 2" | 8 feet |

CPVC also requires specific hanger types -- metal hangers must have a listed protective liner or bushing to prevent the hanger from cutting into the plastic pipe.

## Structural Attachment Methods

### Beam Clamps

Attach to structural steel beams (I-beams, wide flanges, channels):
- C-clamps for I-beam flanges
- Beam clips for lighter structural members
- Must have set screws and retaining straps per NFPA 13
- Load rating must exceed the applied load

### Concrete Inserts

Pre-installed in cast-in-place concrete before pouring:
- Provides the strongest and most reliable attachment
- Must be coordinated with the concrete contractor during pour
- Specified on shop drawings before concrete is placed
- Various styles: slotted channel, threaded rod inserts, coil inserts

### Post-Installed Anchors

Drilled and installed in existing concrete:
- **Expansion anchors** -- expand against the sides of the drilled hole
- **Adhesive (epoxy) anchors** -- threaded rod bonded into the hole with structural adhesive
- Must be listed for fire protection loading
- Installation requires verification of concrete strength and condition
- Pull-test requirements may apply

### Powder-Actuated Fasteners

Fasteners driven into concrete or steel using a powder charge:
- Fast installation
- Limited to specific load ratings
- Not permitted for all applications
- Must be listed for fire protection use
- **Not acceptable for seismic brace attachments** in most jurisdictions

### Wood Screw Attachments

For wood-frame construction:
- Lag screws into structural wood members (joists, beams)
- Must have adequate embedment into sound wood
- Not permitted in the thin edge of manufactured wood products (engineered joists)
- Load capacity depends on wood species, screw size, and embedment depth

## Load Requirements

### Dead Load

Hangers must support the **total weight** of the filled piping system:

| Pipe Size | Weight (Water-Filled, per foot) |
|-----------|-------------------------------|
| 1" Schedule 40 | 2.1 lbs |
| 1-1/2" Schedule 40 | 3.6 lbs |
| 2" Schedule 40 | 5.0 lbs |
| 2-1/2" Schedule 40 | 7.7 lbs |
| 3" Schedule 40 | 10.8 lbs |
| 4" Schedule 40 | 16.3 lbs |
| 6" Schedule 40 | 33.0 lbs |
| 8" Schedule 40 | 52.0 lbs |

### Point Load

NFPA 13 requires that the piping system withstand a **250-pound concentrated load** applied at any point between hangers. This accounts for someone hanging from or standing on the pipe during construction or maintenance.

### Seismic Loads

In seismic zones, hangers and their attachments must also resist horizontal forces calculated per NFPA 13, Chapter 18. Seismic loads are typically 30-50% of the dead load but can be higher depending on the seismic design category.

## Sway Bracing

Sway braces resist horizontal movement of the piping system. They are required:

- In **seismic zones** (SDC C through F depending on pipe size)
- Where horizontal pipe movement could damage the pipe, connections, or the building

### Lateral Braces

Resist movement **perpendicular** to the pipe axis:
- Required on all mains at maximum intervals (typically 40 feet)
- Must be within a specified distance of changes in direction

### Longitudinal Braces

Resist movement **parallel** to the pipe axis:
- Required on all mains at maximum intervals (typically 80 feet)
- Must be within a specified distance of pipe ends and direction changes

### Four-Way Bracing

Required at certain locations where restraint in both horizontal directions is needed:
- Top of risers
- Equipment connections
- Points where differential movement between structural elements occurs

### Brace Hardware

All sway bracing hardware must be:
- **Listed** for fire protection seismic bracing
- Made of **ferrous materials** (steel or iron, not aluminum)
- Connected to the building structure (not to other systems or non-structural elements)
- Coordinated with the structural engineer to verify structural capacity

## Common Hanger and Bracing Errors

1. **Hanger spacing exceeded** -- one of the most common AHJ citations. Verify spacing during installation and before inspection.

2. **Attaching to non-structural elements** -- hangers must attach to the building structure, not to ductwork, ceiling grid, conduit, cable trays, or other building systems.

3. **Missing hangers at direction changes** -- every elbow, tee, and direction change needs a hanger within 12 inches.

4. **Wrong hanger for CPVC** -- metal hangers without protective bushings will damage CPVC pipe over time (stress cracking from contact).

5. **Powder-actuated fasteners for seismic bracing** -- not acceptable. Seismic brace attachments must be through-bolted, welded, or installed with listed anchors.

6. **Insufficient structural capacity** -- the building structure must be able to support the hanger and brace loads. Lightweight steel decking, open-web joists, and engineered wood members have specific load limitations that must be verified.

7. **Missing sway braces after changes** -- when piping is re-routed during coordination, brace locations must be updated. Braces left off the revised drawing are braces that do not get installed.

---

## Coordination with Other Trades

Hanger and brace points must be coordinated with:

- **Structural engineer** -- verify that the structure can support sprinkler loads at the proposed attachment points
- **Mechanical contractor** -- avoid conflicts between sprinkler hangers and ductwork supports
- **Electrical contractor** -- avoid conflicts with conduit supports and cable tray
- **Ceiling contractor** -- hangers must clear the ceiling grid and not interfere with tile installation

In BIM-coordinated projects, hanger and brace locations should be modeled and checked for clashes along with the pipe routing.
