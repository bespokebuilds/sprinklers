Fire sprinkler companies depend on smooth handoffs between departments. A project touches estimating, design, fabrication, the field, and project management -- each with different priorities and timelines. Clear standard operating procedures (SOPs) prevent errors, reduce rework, and keep projects profitable.

---

## Typical Department Structure

### Estimating

Takes the project from bid invitation through contract:
- Reviews plans and specifications
- Performs material takeoff and labor estimation
- Prepares the bid or proposal
- Hands off to project management upon award

**Critical handoff to PM:** The estimate must clearly document assumptions, inclusions, exclusions, and any value engineering suggestions that were included in the bid price.

### Design / Engineering

Produces the technical documents:
- Creates shop drawings from architectural, structural, and MEP plans
- Performs hydraulic calculations
- Submits for permit and coordination review
- Revises based on review comments and field conditions

**Critical handoff to fabrication:** Approved drawings must be clearly marked "Released for Fabrication" with a revision date. Any modifications after release must go through a formal change process.

### Fabrication (Shop)

Prepares physical materials:
- Cuts and prepares pipe per shop drawings
- Threads, grooves, or bevels pipe ends
- Pre-assembles sub-assemblies (riser trim, valve manifolds)
- Labels and stages materials for delivery

**Critical handoff to field:** Materials must be delivered in installation sequence, clearly labeled to match shop drawing piece numbers, with packing lists verified against the bill of materials.

### Field Installation

Installs the system:
- Hangs and connects piping
- Installs sprinkler heads, valves, and devices
- Performs testing (hydrostatic, alarm, supervisory)
- Coordinates with other trades
- Completes trim-out and punch list

**Critical handoff to PM for closeout:** Field must provide completed test certificates, as-built markup drawings, photos of concealed work, and a punch list status report.

### Project Management

Oversees the entire process:
- Manages schedule, budget, and communication
- Processes change orders
- Coordinates between all departments
- Manages the closeout process

## Common Workflow Issues

### Information Gaps Between Estimating and Design

The estimate is often prepared by one person and the design by another. If the design team does not have access to the estimate assumptions, they may design a system that costs more (or less) than what was bid.

**SOP:** The estimator should prepare a **job turnover package** including:
- Marked-up plans showing the approach assumed in the estimate
- System type and head count assumed
- Any value engineering or alternates included
- Special conditions noted during the site visit
- Specification requirements that affect cost (specific manufacturers, testing requirements, etc.)

### Late Design Changes After Fabrication Release

When coordination or field conditions require design changes after materials have been fabricated, the result is wasted material and rework.

**SOP:**
1. All changes after fabrication release must be documented as a **revision**
2. The designer issues a revised drawing with a change cloud and revision note
3. Fabrication verifies which pieces are affected and whether they have been cut
4. Affected pieces are re-fabricated or field-modified as appropriate
5. Cost impact is tracked for potential change order recovery

### Field Conflicts Not Reported

When field crews encounter conflicts with the drawings (ductwork in the way, structural member not where shown, ceiling height different than planned), they sometimes improvise solutions that do not comply with code.

**SOP:**
- All field deviations from shop drawings must be reported to the designer via RFI (Request for Information)
- The designer evaluates the proposed change for code compliance and hydraulic impact
- Approved changes are documented on the as-built drawings
- **No unauthorized field changes** -- this is the single most important SOP in field operations

## Communication Protocols

### RFI (Request for Information)

Used when any department needs clarification:

| From | To | Common RFI Topics |
|------|-----|-------------------|
| Field | Design | Routing conflicts, dimension discrepancies, missing details |
| Design | Architect/Engineer | Ceiling heights, occupancy clarification, structural loading |
| Design | AHJ | Code interpretations, local amendments |
| PM | GC | Schedule changes, access, coordination |

**SOP:** RFIs must be:
- Numbered sequentially
- Documented with the question, response, and date
- Tracked in an RFI log
- Closed out when answered

### Change Order Process

1. Identify the change (field condition, owner request, design change)
2. Document the change with scope description and backup
3. Price the change (materials + labor + overhead + profit)
4. Submit to the PM for review
5. PM submits to the GC or owner for approval
6. **Do not proceed with changed work until the change order is approved** (unless directed in writing by the GC)

### Daily Field Reports

Field foremen should submit daily reports including:
- Crew members on site and hours worked
- Work accomplished (reference shop drawing numbers and areas)
- Issues encountered and how they were resolved
- Equipment used
- Coordination issues or delays caused by other trades
- Safety incidents or near-misses

## Quality Control Checkpoints

### Design Review (Before Submittal)

A second designer or senior reviewer checks:
- [ ] Head spacing and coverage per NFPA 13
- [ ] Pipe sizes match hydraulic calculation requirements
- [ ] Correct head types, K-factors, and temperature ratings specified
- [ ] Hangers and support spacing per code
- [ ] Seismic bracing locations and details (where required)
- [ ] Alarm and supervisory device locations
- [ ] Bill of materials completeness
- [ ] Riser diagram accuracy

### Fabrication Verification (Before Shipping)

Shop quality control checks:
- [ ] Pipe sizes match shop drawings
- [ ] Groove depths verified per manufacturer specs
- [ ] Thread quality and depth verified
- [ ] Correct fittings and pipe schedule used
- [ ] Piece labels match drawing piece numbers
- [ ] All components are listed (UL/FM)

### Field Pre-Inspection (Before AHJ Walk)

The PM or superintendent walks the job:
- [ ] All heads installed per shop drawings (type, orientation, spacing)
- [ ] No painted heads
- [ ] All escutcheons installed
- [ ] Hanger spacing within maximums
- [ ] Control valves accessible and identified with signage
- [ ] Spare head cabinet stocked correctly
- [ ] Test and drain connections functional
- [ ] System signage installed
- [ ] Hydrostatic test completed and documented
- [ ] Alarm devices tested and functional
